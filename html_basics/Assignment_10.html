Special Characters

To display special characters in HTML, you use HTML entities. These entities are a way to encode characters that might otherwise be interpreted as HTML syntax or that are not easily typed on a keyboard. Here are some common special characters and their corresponding HTML entities:

  1. Ampersand (&): '&'
  2. Less than (<): '<'
  3. Greater than (>): '>'
  4. Registered trademark (®): '®'
  5. Copyright (©): '©'
  6. Trademark (™): '™'
